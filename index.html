Research Interests

Cells rely on compartmentalization to organize biomolecules and facilitate biochemical reactions. Traditionally, this refers to membrane-bound organelles such as mitochondria, the endoplasmic reticulum (ER), and lysosomes. However, a growing body of research has highlighted the importance of membrane-less organelles, also known as biomolecular condensates, as fundamental units of cellular organization. These dynamic compartments, often formed through liquid–liquid phase separation, represent an exciting frontier in cell biology.

Biomolecular condensates play diverse roles in both physiological and pathological contexts. Our lab investigates how condensates and phase separation contribute to fundamental biological processes such as small RNA–based epigenetic inheritance and stress responses, as well as their roles in diseases like cancer. Ultimately, we aim to develop therapeutic strategies by targeting or modulating phase separation and condensate dynamics.

Current Research Focus

  1. Small RNA–Mediated Transgenerational Epigenetic Inheritance
    We explore how non-DNA-based epigenetic information is passed from parents to offspring via small RNAs, shaping gene expression across generations.

  2. Biomolecular Condensates and Phase Separation
    We study the formation, regulation, and function of biomolecular condensates in both normal and disease states, including their roles in stress response, epigenetic inheritance, and cancer.

  3. Biotechnology and Synthetic Biology
    We develop and apply innovative tools to investigate condensates and phase separation, and to harness these mechanisms for potential therapeutic applications.

Join Our Lab

We are always looking for enthusiastic, self-motivated individuals who are excited about science and eager to explore the frontiers of biomolecular condensates, phase separation, and epigenetic inheritance.

Whether you're a postdoctoral researcher, PhD student, master’s student, or undergraduate with a strong interest in molecular biology, cell biology, RNA biology, or biophysics—we welcome you to apply!

In our lab, you will have the opportunity to:

  • Tackle fundamental questions in cell and developmental biology
  • Gain hands-on experience with cutting-edge technologies
  • Work in a supportive, collaborative, and intellectually stimulating environment
